Body mass index (BMI) is a value derived from the mass (weight) and height of a person. The BMI is defined as the body mass in kilograms divided by the square of the body height in meters, and is expressed in units of kg/m2. The BMI may be determined using a table[note 1] or chart which displays BMI as a function of mass and height using contour lines or colours for different BMI categories, and which may use other units of measurement (converted to metric units for the calculation).[note 2] The BMI is a convenient rule of thumb used to broadly categorize a person as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese based on tissue mass (muscle, fat, and bone) and height.
